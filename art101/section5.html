R E A D I N G

It’s difficult because most of the technology we use nowadays has been condensed down to a phone. Maybe the voice at a self checkout line? No one really needs to be told to take their items or needs to hear thank you from a robot voice, but it makes the checkout process feel more “right.” Maybe also the tools in Photoshop could be an example? All the brushes and tools and even stuff like masking and dodging and burning are emulated after real world tools and processes.

Gesamtkunstwerk is a term that means uses a synthesis of different forms to be able to appeal to different senses. For instance, something could be not only visually appealing, but also sound really pleasant. In particular, phones must not only look good, but they have to have those satisfying clicks and also be physically comfortable to use.

Sounds can be really satisfying, so it’s important that a device sounds pleasant. There also needs to be some sort of feedback so people know their device is functioning.

Skeuomorphism is more modeling a design after something that’s already familiar. In a way it’s minimal because it’s taking the thinking out of everything by presenting something that is already familiar and that already blends into the environment even if it is objectively complex. Flat Design is something that is minimal in every sense, being not noticeable by taking up the fewest visual resources and being as simple and plain as possible.

F I N A L

Click to play!



This game is very basic compared to what I intended, but I think it's a decent first go at it. I make the design as "flat" as possible, keeping it down to 3 colors with only a little bit of transparency and no shadows, blurs or highlights. I also tried to avoid using any text or direct instructions. I attempted to make the game intuitive enough to figure out without a need for the controls to be spelled out. I used pleasant sounds to give the user a rewarded feeling and tried to give graphical clues to guide behavior.
This game usees the libraries p5.play, p5.scenemanager, and p5.sound, in addition to p5.js, of course.
