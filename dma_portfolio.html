DUSK LILY

Fantasy painting inspired by daily scenery of the Emiquon National Wildlife Refuge...

DUSK LILY | Photoshop

CURIOUS | photoshop

STORM

Fantasy painting inspired by Midwestern autumn storms.

STORM | photoshop



Perhaps this isn't something born-and-raised Californians can relate much to, but I've always been really afraid of storms, so I wanted to paint something that expressed how small and helpless I feel when a huge storm is in the area. I felt that thunder and wind would be appropriately represented by a huge galloping horse taking shape in the clouds, which dwarf a house and tree over which they're passing.

The image was painted in Adobe Photoshop.

BALANCE

Animated digital paintings expressing the difficulty of balancing priorities as an artist.

BALANCE | photoshop & live2d & html5 & javascript & css


(Click image to view site.)

WARNING: Might not work on mobile. High data usage.

This work is an expression of the difficulties of balancing priorities and direction as an artist. The initial portrait aims to splice together the areas of leisure and personal interest, conceptual and self-expression, learning and technical skill, and work and wide appeal into a disjointed portrait. Each piece can then be expanded by the viewer to explore another work created with each of these areas in mind. The work itself also attempts to achieve this balance in its creation through the stylistic and color preferences of the creator, learning a new program, the wide appeal of a feminine figure, and the expression of this overall concept.

The characters were painted in Adobe Photoshop, animated in Cubism Live2D, and assembled on the web using Javascript, HTML5, and CSS.

OBITUARY CLIPPINGS AND 1040-EZS

An exploration of life through paperwork and a statement on the tenuous nature of lifes expectations and the validity of different life paths.

OBITUARY CLIPPINGS AND 1040-EZS | blender & photoshop & premiere

This brief animation is intended to be displayed on a wall to mimic papers falling around the viewer. It depicts a potential "normal" life seen through various pieces of paper. The paper is a symbol of the fragile and tenuous nature of life's circumstances. The work and title have been created with the quote, "In this world nothing can be said to be certain, except death and taxes." Overall, it's a statement that lives that deviate from society's expectations are no less valid for it.

The content was found through various forms and documents and edited to remove any information. Each paper sheet was then created using Blender and a plugin for it called "Import Images as Planes." I then warped each sheet, created a back and forth animation, and put each into its own particle emitter. After rendering the animation in Blender, I added the paper sounds with Adobe Premiere.

TIMEPIECE

A set of images rendered completely in Photoshop mimicking the look of tradition media.

TIMEPIECE | Photoshop









Time Piece is a set of 4 drawings, all drawn with a graphics tablet in Photoshop. They use a brush that mimics the appearance of graphite and techniques and values that attempt to convincingly recreate pencil, ballpoint pen, charcoal, and fine tip marker respectively. The set is a light-hearted fantasty portrait of a fairy character and her pet who manipulate time.

A SELF PORTRAIT IN TEN PARTS FOR TEN AUDIENCES

Work exploring the idea of the multiple selves we present to different people and the threads that unify them.

A SELF PORTRAIT IN TEN PARTS FOR TEN AUDIENCES | digital photography





















I've always hated my appearance and by extension self portraits, but self portraits are unavoidable as an art student. This project sees the self as the actual inner person rather than their appearance and attempts to portray the artist through objects that describe interests, hobbies and personality traits. It uses the concept of multiple selves that are adapted and presented for different interactions and relationships with different people. This set of photos was taken with a DSLR camera and edited in Adobe Lightroom and Photoshop to isolate the pink hues in each photo.

CAT-CHAPON!

A net art commentary on predatory gambling in gaming.

CAT-CHAPON | html & javascript

Click to...


This net art website is about the current state of gaming, especially the free to play game model, which has become overrun with unethical gambling practices in the form of loot boxes and gachapons. while it's a little visually rough around the edges, this project taught me a lot about Javascript.

The site allows you to spend imaginary currency to randomly draw different cat pictures. A catalog documents which cat pictures you've collected to push people to try to collect the whole set. The "shop," where a user might purchase more of this currency with real money, chides the user for taking this route.

Website was created with Javascript, HTML5, and CSS. Cat images all sourced from free stock image sites. Decorative text was created in Photoshop.
