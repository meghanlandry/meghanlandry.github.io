BALANCE

animated digital paintings expressing the difficulty of balancing priorities as an artist

BALANCE | photoshop & live2d & html5 & javascript & css


WARNING: Might not work on mobile. High data usage.

Click to view site.

This work is an expression of the difficulties of balancing priorities and direction as an artist. The initial portrait aims to splice together the areas of leisure and personal interest, conceptual and self-expression, learning and technical skill, and work and wide appeal into a disjointed portrait. Each piece can then be expanded by the viewer to explore another work created with each of these areas in mind. The work itself also attempts to achieve this balance in its creation through the stylistic and color preferences of the creator, learning a new program, the wide appeal of a feminine figure, and the expression of this overall concept.

OBITUARY CLIPPINGS AND 1040-EZS

An exploration of life through paperwork and a statement on the tenuous nature of lifes expectations and the validity of different life paths...

OBITUARY CLIPPINGS AND 1040-EZS | blender & photoshop & premiere

This brief animation is intended to be displayed on a wall to mimic papers falling around the viewer. It depicts a potential "normal" life seen through various pieces of paper. The paper is a symbol of the fragile and tenuous nature of life's circumstances. The work and title have been created with the quote, "In this world nothing can be said to be certain, except death and taxes." Overall, it's a statement that lives that deviate from society's expectations are no less valid for it.

N O I S E

a oberservation of the temptation to ignore reality in favor of digitally enhanced entertainment as devices become more spectacular than life...

N O I S E | digital video & photoshop & premiere

This video is a critique on the use of video as a distraction from real problems in the world. It highlights that as environmental problems get worse, technology becomes more spectacular, perhaps lessening the perceived value of reality. The screens progress from an old projector through television and up to modern technology, and the screen grows larger and obscures an ever larger portion of the dying natural background.
