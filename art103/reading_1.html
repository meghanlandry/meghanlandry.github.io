Q1. -- What is a good working definition of what a System is and perhaps what it is not?

A system is a group of things and processes that are interdependent. If something functions on its own with relying on or affecting something else, then it isnâ€™t part of a system.

Q2. -- For the 4 system definitions you were to look up please give in your own words what they mean and give an example of them for each case.

Modularity - when parts of a system can be moved around
ex: a home built PC
Decomposability - limit of functional difference
ex: piece of confetti
Emergence - the result of a functioning system
ex: computer display
Chaos Theory - idea that a lot of different things can happen based on a small change
ex: pinball machine

Q3 -- Give us your definition of what Tessellation means and give an example you encountered, not mentioned in the reading.

Tessellation is when a bunch of little pieces fit together in a pattern. ex: pixels, bricks

Q4 -- What is The difference between ideas of Modularity and Tessellation? What are the properties that are in opposition with each other?

Tessellation all fits together and forms a pattern while Modularity is not required to do this. Modular pieces can also be moved around freely, whereas pieces of a Tessellation may break the whole pattern if moved around to an incorrect spot.

Q5 -- What is the difference between designing something that has Complexity (aperiodic) vs being Uniform (periodic).

Uniform designs are repeated and the same thing over and over. Complex things have more variation and do not need to repeat exactly, unpredictable.
